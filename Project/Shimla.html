Shimla Hill


Shimla Hill (or Shimla Pahari in the local dialect) is a hill of the Sherwan range extending to the west of Abbottabad, Pakistan. It is not to be confused with Simla / Shimla in Himachal Pradesh, India. The latter place's name derives from the name of the Hindu deity Shyamala Devi, whilst the former is derived from the Hindko (Punjabi) word Shamla or Shumla, that is, the crest of a turban, since the Sherwan range at this point comes up to Abbottabad in the shape of a crest, or ridge.

Shimla Hill Abbottabad or (Shimla Pahari) is a most popular picnic spot among travel lovers, covered with a beautiful green pine forest, located near Abbottabad, Pakistan. It's about 10 kilometers drive away from the main Abbottabad city. At Shimla Hill, most of the time visitors come to enjoy in the month of November and January, because of its cold and frosty climate, families, tourists and visitors also enjoying, hiking tracks, walking at Shimla Hill station in the top of the mountains, and can captured the eye catching views of Abbottabad city in their cameras.
